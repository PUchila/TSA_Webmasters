Challenge documentation

About the Texas Directory Hub

This project was built for a national student technology competition and structured like a real public-service platform: clear navigation, credible sources, and accessibility-first design.

Problem

People often need help quickly—food, housing support, health care, crisis services, or job resources—but the information is scattered across many websites. That fragmentation creates a “search burden,” which is especially harmful when someone is stressed, lacks transportation, has limited internet access, or needs support outside normal business hours.

Audience

Individuals & families seeking support.

Gatekeepers

Schools & counselors who share resources.

Providers

Nonprofits & agencies serving communities.

Our Solution

The Texas Directory Hub organizes trusted starting points into a single directory with fast search, filters, and a statewide map. Instead of forcing users to guess where to go, the hub provides a clear path: pick a category, narrow by location, read a concise summary, then go directly to the official resource.

  • Trust-first design: each card links to the organization’s official site.
  • Low-friction discovery: search + category + city + ZIP filters reduce time-to-answer.
  • Map + list: supports both visual and text-based browsing styles.
  • Community growth: “Submit Resource” expands coverage while keeping a review step.

How It Works

  1. Directory dataset: resources are stored with category, city, ZIP, description, and coordinates for mapping.
  2. Filtering: users can narrow results instantly without page reloads.
  3. Mapping: markers are clustered for readability; popups provide quick context and a direct link.
  4. Submission workflow: the form drafts an email to a review inbox to support human verification before publishing.

Impact

Impact is measured by reducing time-to-find and improving confidence in the next step. The hub prioritizes usability on mobile (where many users search), accessibility for keyboard/screen reader users, and clear calls to action.

Example scenario: A student counselor needs food and housing assistance resources in under 2 minutes. With category + city filters and direct links, the counselor can produce vetted options quickly and share them with a family.

Future Improvements (Scalability)

  • Verification pipeline: add review statuses (pending/verified/needs update) and periodic link checks to prevent stale entries.
  • Expanded coverage: include more counties, rural areas, and community clinics; partner with local agencies for submissions.
  • Multilingual access: add Spanish-first browsing and plain-language summaries for wider reach.
  • Accessibility upgrades: offer “high contrast” mode and an “easy read” view for cognitive accessibility.
  • Integration: export to an API or spreadsheet pipeline so school districts and nonprofits can embed the directory.
